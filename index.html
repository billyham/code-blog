A little about me

I am an iOS and JavaScript developer. With a background in filmmaking and graphic design, I am enthusiastic about making beautiful, responsive applications. This portfolio site provides highlights of my work as a developer, while this biography describes some of my activities outside of coding.

I write, direct and produce my own short films. The term that best describes my film work: Romantic Macabredy. Filmmaking is a delightful creative outlet and it has lead to some unique opportunities. For eight years I ran a film festival in Seattle – Northwest Film Forum's Local Sightings Film Festival. I participated in a two month artist residency in Belfast, Northern Ireland. Learning the history and meeting the people led me to make new films through that cultural lens. A year later, I initiated a film curation and exhibition project in Skopje, Macedonia. My travel there helped to create a bridge between video artists in Southeastern Europe and the Pacific Northwest.

At my job and in my personal hobbies I pursue creative endeavors at the intersection of art and technology. I have a passion for physical computing – creating hybrid projects that incorporate software development – and hardware hacking – deconstructing things like cameras and lenses to explore their inner workings and altering their function. I am a lens hacker and I teach workshops on making homemade lenses for DSLR cameras.

I have a history of working in academic environments, specifically in managing equipment facilities and digital workspaces for film schools. My role involves creating a professional environment, a space where students feel welcome and are willing to ask questions regardless of their level of technical sophistication. Students are best served when a real sense of community is made available to them. To that end I have actively produced programs to support artists. I produced dozens of panel discussions with filmmakers and industry professionals. After obtaining sponsorship from Kodak I created a program that commissioned two dozen artists to make original short films and I managed grant programs that awarded artists with funding and in-kind services.

Making and hacking is present in my hobbies but it also spills over into my job. I am constantly creating projects for myself to improve the efficiency of my workplace systems and to spark the imagination of students. Some of those projects are featured on this site and include a home-brew device for transferring film to digital video (as an affordable option for students and indie filmmakers) and a custom iPad app for managing equipment inventory and scheduling.
